Celebrating Christian Festivals

Celebrating faith, hope, and the timeless traditions that bring millions together across the world.

Christian Festivals

Christmas

Christmas celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ in Bethlehem. Christians believe that God sent His Son to Earth to bring salvation to humanity.

View history

Easter

Easter commemorates the resurrection of Jesus from the dead, three days after His crucifixion.Victory over death, eternal life, and salvation.

View history

Palm Sunday

Palm Sunday takes place one week before Easter and marks the beginning of Holy Week. Churches often distribute palm leaves to worshippers

View history

Pentecost

Pentecost occurs 50 days after Easter and celebrates the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the apostles and other followers of Jesus.

View history

Epiphany

In many cultures, Epiphany is marked by special church services, blessing of homes, and the sharing of traditional foods.

View history

Advent

Churches light a candle on an Advent wreath each week, and many people engage in prayer, fasting, and acts of kindness.

View history

Good Friday

Good Friday commemorates the crucifixion and death of Jesus Christ at Calvary. It is observed on the Friday before Easter and is a solemn day of reflection.

View history

All Saints’ Day

All Saints’ Day is celebrated on honors all saints, known and unknown, who have lived faithful Christian lives.Many Christians and visit graves of loved ones on this day.
