One World, Many Festivals

"Festivals unite hearts, celebrating our shared joy and diverse traditions."

Eid-ul-Fitr

Eid al-Fitr, or the “Festival of Breaking the Fast,” marks the end of Ramadan.It is a time of joy, gratitude, and unity.

Eid-ul-Adha

It honors the unwavering faith and obedience of Prophet Ibrahim (A.S), who was willing to sacrifice his beloved son Ismail (A.S) as an act of submission to God.

Christmas

Christmas commemorates the birth of Jesus Christ in Bethlehem. It is a joyful time marked by prayers and family gatherings.

Easter

Easter celebrates the resurrection of Jesus from the dead, three days after His crucifixion. It is the most important festival in Christianity.

Good Friday

It is a solemn day of remembrance, fasting, and prayer, reflecting on the sacrifice Jesus made for humanity.

Pentecost

Celebrated 50 days after Easter, Pentecost commemorates the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the apostles.

Epiphany

Epiphany, also known as Three Kings’ Day, is celebrated on January 6th. It commemorates the visit of the Magi (Wise Men) to the infant Jesus.

Palm Sunday

Palm Sunday marks the beginning of Holy Week Jesus’ triumphant entry into Jerusalem, where crowds welcomed Him with palm branches.

Advent

Advent is a sacred season of preparation and anticipation, observed in the four weeks before Christmas.

All Saints' Day

It is a time to remember those who dedicated their lives to God and now dwell in His eternal presence.

Vaisakhi

It is both a religious and cultural festival, observed with prayers, Nagar Kirtans, and community gatherings.

Guru Nanak Gurpurab

Guru Nanak Gurpurab celebrates the birth of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder of Sikhism. It is the most important Sikh festival.

Guru Gobind Singh Jayanti

This day honors the birth of Guru Gobind Singh Ji. The festival includes kirtan, poetry, and remembrance of his teachings.

Hola Mohalla

Sikhs gather at Anandpur Sahib for spectacular displays of Gatka (martial arts), mock battles, and kirtan.

Guru Arjan Dev Ji Shaheedi Divas

This day honors the martyrdom of Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the 5th Guru of Sikhism, who was tortured to death.

Guru Ram Das Jayanti

He composed many of the hymns found in the Guru Granth Sahib and is remembered for promoting service, compassion etc.

Maghi

Maghi is celebrated in memory of the forty Sikh martyrs (Chali Mukte) who sacrificed their lives fighting for Guru Gobind Singh Ji.

Bandi Chhor Divas

On this auspicious day, Guru Hargobind Sahib Ji secured the release of 52 innocent kings from unjust imprisonment and returned to Amritsar .

Magha Puja

A day to honor the spontaneous gathering of 1,250 enlightened monks to hear the Buddha’s teachings.

Parinirvana Day

Commemorates the passing of the Buddha into final Nirvana.

Vesak

The most sacred day in Buddhism, celebrating the birth, enlightenment, and passing of the Buddha.

Asalha Puja

Marks the Buddha’s first sermon — the turning of the Wheel of Dharma.

Songkran

The traditional Buddhist New Year, especially celebrated in Thailand. A joyful festival of water, cleansing and family.

Uposatha

A day of intensified practice, observed regularly by laypeople and monastics. Devoted to meditation, ethical reflection, and spiritual cleansing .

Kathina Ceremony

A time of offering robes and necessities to the monastic community after the rains retreat.

Loy Krathong

A beautiful festival of light and letting go, where candle-lit offerings float on water.

Diwali

Celebrates the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Homes are decorated with diyas, rangoli, and fireworks.

Holi

Marks the arrival of spring. People play with colors, water, and enjoy festive sweets like gujiya.

Navratri

9 nights of worshipping Goddess Durga’s different forms. Ends with Dussehra — the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana.

Janmashtami

Birth of Lord Krishna. Celebrated with fasting, midnight celebrations, and Dahi Handi in Maharashtra.

Raksha Bandhan

Sister ties rakhi on brother’s wrist, symbolizing love and protection. Brother gives gifts and vows to protect her.

Makar Sankranti

Marks sun’s transition into Capricorn. Celebrated with kite flying, sesame sweets, and prayers.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Birth of Lord Ganesha. Grand idols installed, worshipped, and later immersed in water with processions.

Ram Navami

Celebrates birth of Lord Rama. Devotees read Ramayana, sing bhajans, and visit temples.

Our Mission

"Our religions may be different, but festivals bring all hearts together. Whether it’s the lights of Diwali, the sweetness of Eid, the joy of Christmas, or the spirit of Gurpurab — every festival is a chance to celebrate together."
